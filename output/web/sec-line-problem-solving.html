    Subsection 4.1.1 Finding the Slope of a Line

    The slope (or gradient) of a line measures its steepness. Given two points \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\text{,}\) the slope is
    \begin{equation*} m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}. \end{equation*}
    You can also read slope directly from the equation \(y = mx + c\text{,}\) or use \(m = -\dfrac{a}{b}\) when the line is in the form \(ax + by + c = 0\text{.}\)

    Subsection 4.1.3 Distance and Midpoint

    \begin{equation*} d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}. \end{equation*}

    \begin{equation*} \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\; \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right). \end{equation*}
