    Section 3.9 When a Circle Touches an Axis

    Circle Tangent to an Axis.

    If a circle with centre \((h, k)\) and radius \(r\) is:
    ðŸ”—
    Tangent to the \(x\)-axis: then \(|k| = r\text{.}\) The point of tangency is \((h, 0)\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    Tangent to the \(y\)-axis: then \(|h| = r\text{.}\) The point of tangency is \((0, k)\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    ðŸ”—
    These follow from Theorem 20: the tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact. Since the axes are horizontal/vertical, the radius to the tangent point must be vertical/horizontal.
